Skin Conditions

Learn about common skin conditions and how to manage them

Common Skin Conditions

Skin conditions can affect people of all ages. Understanding these conditions can help you identify symptoms and seek appropriate treatment. Below are some of the most common skin conditions and their characteristics.

What is Acne?

Acne is a skin condition that occurs when hair follicles become plugged with oil and dead skin cells, leading to whiteheads, blackheads, or pimples.

Symptoms:

  • Whiteheads (closed plugged pores)
  • Blackheads (open plugged pores)
  • Papules (small red, tender bumps)
  • Pustules (papules with pus at their tips)
  • Nodules (large, solid, painful lumps beneath the surface)
  • Cystic lesions (painful, pus-filled lumps beneath the surface)

Treatment Options:

  • Over-the-counter topical treatments (benzoyl peroxide, salicylic acid)
  • Prescription topical medications (retinoids, antibiotics)
  • Oral medications (antibiotics, isotretinoin)
  • Procedures (chemical peels, light therapy)

What is Eczema?

Eczema is a condition where patches of skin become inflamed, itchy, red, cracked, and rough. It's often related to an overactive immune response to irritants.

Symptoms:

  • Dry, sensitive skin
  • Intense itching
  • Red, inflamed skin
  • Rough, leathery, or scaly patches
  • Oozing or crusting
  • Areas of swelling

Treatment Options:

  • Moisturizers
  • Topical corticosteroids
  • Topical calcineurin inhibitors
  • Antihistamines for itching
  • Phototherapy
  • Identifying and avoiding triggers

What is Psoriasis?

Psoriasis is a chronic autoimmune condition that causes rapid buildup of skin cells, resulting in scaling on the skin's surface.

Symptoms:

  • Red patches of skin covered with thick, silvery scales
  • Small scaling spots (commonly seen in children)
  • Dry, cracked skin that may bleed
  • Itching, burning, or soreness
  • Thickened, pitted, or ridged nails
  • Swollen and stiff joints

Treatment Options:

  • Topical treatments (corticosteroids, vitamin D analogues)
  • Light therapy
  • Oral or injected medications
  • Biologics

What is Rosacea?

Rosacea is a chronic skin condition that causes redness and visible blood vessels in your face, sometimes producing small, red, pus-filled bumps.

Symptoms:

  • Facial redness
  • Swollen red bumps
  • Eye problems (dry, irritated, swollen eyes and eyelids)
  • Enlarged nose
  • Visible blood vessels

Treatment Options:

  • Topical medications to reduce redness
  • Oral antibiotics
  • Laser therapy
  • Avoiding triggers (spicy foods, alcohol, extreme temperatures)

What is Contact Dermatitis?

Contact dermatitis is a red, itchy rash caused by direct contact with a substance or an allergic reaction to it.

Symptoms:

  • Red rash
  • Itching
  • Dry, cracked, scaly skin
  • Bumps and blisters, sometimes with oozing and crusting
  • Swelling, burning, or tenderness

Treatment Options:

  • Identifying and avoiding the cause
  • Over-the-counter anti-itch creams
  • Calamine lotion
  • Oral antihistamines
  • Corticosteroid creams

When to See a Dermatologist

While many skin conditions can be managed with over-the-counter treatments, it's important to know when to seek professional help. Consider seeing a dermatologist if:

  • Your skin condition is causing significant discomfort or pain
  • Over-the-counter treatments aren't working
  • Your skin condition is affecting your daily activities or sleep
  • You notice any changes in the appearance of moles or skin growths
  • You have a family history of skin cancer
  • You have a skin condition that keeps coming back or worsening
