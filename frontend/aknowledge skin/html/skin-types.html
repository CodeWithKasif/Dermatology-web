Understanding Skin Types

Discover your skin type and learn how to care for it properly with our comprehensive guide.

What Determines Your Skin Type?

Your skin type is determined by genetics, hormones, environment, and lifestyle factors. Understanding your skin type is the first step to developing an effective skincare routine.

Skin types can change over time due to aging, hormonal shifts, seasonal changes, and other factors. It's important to reassess your skin periodically and adjust your skincare routine accordingly.

Normal Skin

Normal skin is well-balanced - neither too oily nor too dry. It has good circulation, a smooth texture, and a healthy complexion with no severe sensitivity.

Characteristics

  • Balanced oil production
  • Small, barely visible pores
  • Smooth, even texture
  • Good blood circulation
  • No severe sensitivity
  • Few imperfections
  • Radiant complexion

Care Tips

  • Gentle cleansing twice daily
  • Regular exfoliation (1-2 times per week)
  • Lightweight, balanced moisturizer
  • Daily sun protection
  • Antioxidant serums to maintain skin health

Dry Skin

Dry skin produces less sebum than normal skin, leading to a lack of moisture and natural oils needed for skin protection. This can result in a tight, rough feeling and visible flaking.

Characteristics

  • Almost invisible pores
  • Dull, rough complexion
  • Red patches
  • Less elasticity
  • More visible lines
  • Flaking, scaling, or peeling
  • Feels tight, especially after cleansing

Care Tips

  • Gentle, cream-based cleansers
  • Avoid hot water
  • Rich, oil-based moisturizers
  • Hydrating serums with hyaluronic acid
  • Gentle exfoliation (1-2 times per week)
  • Humidifier in dry environments
  • Avoid alcohol-based products

Oily Skin

Oily skin produces excess sebum, giving the skin a shiny, greasy appearance. While this extra oil can help protect the skin and prevent early signs of aging, it can also lead to clogged pores and acne.

Characteristics

  • Enlarged, clearly visible pores
  • Shiny, thick complexion
  • Blackheads, pimples, or other blemishes
  • Skin appears plump and thick
  • Less prone to wrinkling
  • Makeup doesn't stay on well
  • Prone to acne breakouts

Care Tips

  • Gentle foaming cleansers
  • Oil-free, non-comedogenic products
  • Lightweight, water-based moisturizers
  • Regular exfoliation with BHAs (salicylic acid)
  • Clay masks to absorb excess oil
  • Blotting papers throughout the day
  • Don't over-wash (can increase oil production)

Combination Skin

Combination skin features areas that are both oily and dry. The T-zone (forehead, nose, and chin) is typically oily, while the cheeks and other areas are normal to dry.

Characteristics

  • Oily T-zone (forehead, nose, chin)
  • Enlarged pores in the T-zone
  • Normal to dry cheeks
  • Blackheads in oily areas
  • Shiny skin by midday
  • Dry patches on cheeks or jaw
  • Seasonal changes affect skin balance

Care Tips

  • Balanced pH cleansers
  • Zone-specific treatments
  • Lightweight, balanced moisturizers
  • Hydrating serums for dry areas
  • Oil-control products for T-zone
  • Multi-masking (different masks for different areas)
  • Gentle exfoliation 1-2 times per week

Sensitive Skin

Sensitive skin reacts easily to products, environmental factors, or both. It may be prone to redness, itching, burning, and discomfort.

Characteristics

  • Reacts easily to products
  • Prone to redness and flushing
  • May sting or burn
  • Easily irritated by environmental factors
  • May have visible broken capillaries
  • Prone to rashes and bumps
  • Can be dry, oily, or combination

Care Tips

  • Fragrance-free, hypoallergenic products
  • Minimal ingredient lists
  • Patch test new products
  • Gentle, non-foaming cleansers
  • Physical sunscreens (zinc oxide, titanium dioxide)
  • Soothing ingredients (aloe, oat, centella asiatica)
  • Avoid hot water, harsh exfoliants, and alcohol
